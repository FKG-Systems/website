Capabilities

Engineering the whole mission loop.

Airframes are only the visible part. The useful aircraft is the stack around it: power, avionics, payload, autonomy, telemetry, operators and a test culture that catches mistakes before the sky does.

01

UAV platform design

Airframe layout, propulsion matching, battery planning, landing gear, payload bays and maintainable mechanical packaging.

02

Avionics integration

Flight controllers, radios, GNSS, power distribution, ESCs, wiring harnesses, sensor placement and bench-level validation.

03

Autonomy & ground systems

Mission planning, telemetry workflows, ground control procedures, failsafe thinking and operator interfaces that stay readable under pressure.

04

Flight test culture

Pre-flight checklists, controlled iteration, post-flight data review, repair loops and practical field discipline. Not glamorous. Very important.

Aircraft and payloads

From carbon tubes to mission data.

FKG Systems treats unmanned aircraft as complete systems, not just frames with motors attached. Structure, battery, propulsion, control, radio link and payload all need to agree with each other — a small aviation parliament, basically, but with more soldering.

  • Multirotor and experimental UAV platforms
  • Payload mounting and power budgeting
  • Telemetry, command links and mission procedures
  • Ground-test-first development rhythm

Workshop discipline

Read the manual. Check the connector. Then fly.

The fastest way to build a reliable aircraft is not bravado; it is careful integration. FKGAL Aviation’s workbench culture is visible in the details: labeling, harness routing, service access, prop safety, repeatable setup and honest test notes.
